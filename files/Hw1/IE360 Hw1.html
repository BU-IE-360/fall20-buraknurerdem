Now, before commenting on the graph, it should be noted that the trend percentages' means and the spending percentages differ in nature. What I mean is that the ways that those two datasets calculated probably doesn't allow them to be compared directly. It is arguable whether they should be plotted on the same graph. I plotted anyway for visual purposes.

Google search results of rental housing and for sale housing show increasing trends, while expenditure percentage of housing and renting are fairly constant over time. My opinion on this subject is that since housing is such a basic need, for its percantage to change drastically over time there has to be a national or global economic boom or crisis. It is not suprising that it is fairly constant. However, increasing trends of search results show us, in my opinion, that the medium of looking for houses to buy or rent has moved to internet over time with the help of widespreading usage of technology and internet. It looks like people started to prefer the internet to search for suitable housing compared to old methods such as touring neighborhoods and calling numbers on the windows or consulting real estate offices.
